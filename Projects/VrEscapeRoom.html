About This Project

Technology Used:
Unity Game Engine, C# scripting, XR Interaction Toolkit, VR controllers, teleport locomotion, Apartment Kit asset pack for environment building, and Oculus Quest 2 for testing and gameplay recording.

Features:
A VR escape room set inside a realistic apartment with four main puzzles. Players interact with objects using VR controllers (grab, place, unlock, enter codes). The rooms include a hidden-key puzzle, keypad puzzle, object placement challenge, and a riddle-based final puzzle. The environment is designed for immersion with hand interactions, physics-based objects, and natural VR movement.

Target Audience:
Casual to experienced VR players, puzzle lovers, curious explorers, and individuals who enjoy mystery, escape rooms, and interactive storytelling. Age group 16–35. Includes player types such as puzzle solvers, explorers, and speed runners who enjoy immersive VR challenges.

Challenges Faced:
Setting up working VR interactions, configuring colliders and triggers, handling object grabbing and placement accuracy, creating puzzle logic that connects between rooms, debugging VR controllers not interacting correctly, keeping the environment realistic and readable, and optimizing the scene to run smoothly in VR without lag or motion sickness.
